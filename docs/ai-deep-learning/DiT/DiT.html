What is a Diffusion Transformer (DiT)?

Diffusion Transformer (DiT) is a class of diffusion models that are based on the transformer architecture. Developed by William Peebles at UC Berkeley and Saining Xie at New York University, DiT aims to improve the performance of diffusion models by replacing the commonly used U-Net backbone with a transformer.

Introduction to Diffusion Models

Diffusion models are a type of generative model that simulates a Markov chain to transition from a simple prior distribution to the data distribution. Each step is a small random walk. This is why they are called “diffusion” models.

Convolutional U-NET Architecture

The U-Net architecture is a type of convolutional neural network (CNN) that was developed for biomedical image segmentation. The architecture is designed like a U-shape, hence the name U-Net.

Convolutional U-NET Backbone: Disadvantages

  • U-NET often struggle with capturing long-range dependencies and global context in the input data

  • Convolution operation in U-NETs is translation invariant, which means it treats a feature the same regardless of its position in the image.

卷积没有位置的概念，在一张图片中即不管位置是否一样，只要特征一样就认为是相同的 难以抓取长范围的依赖和全局的上下文，必须依赖更深的网络和更大的过滤器，会导致网络很大

Shifting towards Transformer Backbone

Unlike convolutional networks, transformers can model long-range dependencies without the need for deep networks or large filters. Transformers are not translation invariant, which means they can capture the absolute position of features.
